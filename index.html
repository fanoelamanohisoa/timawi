about

The company has many experiences both on the preparation and conditioning of vanilla since its creation in 1999 until today. In 2002, she embarked on the phase of an integrated and well organized production: from the culture to the management of the peasant productions by joining an association.

In 2004, production continues to grow and business size is changing at the same time. Illustration of production techniques and all phases of vanilla preparation.

The method of preparation described below, known as the "Bourbon method", has been adopted mainly by all the producing countries. After picking the vanilla preparation consists of a series of 6 operations. Each phase is important.

All phases of vanilla preparation

SCALDING

This operation involves immersing baskets of osier containing green vanilla pods in water heated to 65 °. The immersion lasts about 3 minutes and allows the mortification of the pods.

STEAMING

The pods are then placed in large padded blankets wooden crates to avoid heat loss for 12 hours (steaming or sweating). It is at this stage that the vanilla takes on its chocolate color, the catalysis of the fermentation allowing the subsequent development of the characteristic vanilla fragrance.

DRYING

It is done in the sun and in the shade. The pods are arranged on racks. If sun drying only lasts one to two weeks, drying in the shade after sorting lasts more than one month.

THE TRUNK

The pods are then put in wooden trunks lined with parchment paper for eight months. The perfume appears late, it is refined during this period. Trunks are checked weekly to remove moldy pods.

CALIBRATION

The pods are measured and classified one by one in "split" "not split" "black and red". The length and the color determine the value of the vanilla. Pods of the same length and classification are usually tied together and grouped into booties. Each boot weighs about 250 grams (70 to 100 pods), the weight and number of pods vary depending on the qualities.

PACKAGING

The pods are packed in tinplate boxes or reinforced cardboard lined with parchment paper.
